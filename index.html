SUITS-VLM-Bench

A benchmark for evaluating vision-language model performance on astronaut state detection during Extravehicular Activity. Images from the NASA SUITS program are augmented across three EVA operational phases and used to probe model accuracy and confidence calibration under controlled distribution shift.

Benchmark Design

Three-Phase EVA Classification

Each image is labeled with one of three EVA operational phases. The VLM predicts the phase and returns a confidence score. Augmented variants stress-test robustness to lighting change, orientation shift, occlusion, and scale variation.

PHASE 01

EGRESS

Astronaut exits the airlock and transitions to the external environment. Equipment checks, suit pressurization, and initial positioning are key visual indicators.

suit-checkairlockclose-rangesetup

PHASE 02

NAVIGATION

Crew traversing surface or worksite. Navigation panel interaction, ANAV block/RTH commands, LiDAR and comms status visible from bird's-eye and close-up views.

nav-panelanavbirds-eyelidar

PHASE 03

LTV REPAIR

Lunar Terrain Vehicle maintenance. Fuse box access, science management systems, PDD, and component-level hardware interaction distinguish this phase.

fuse-boxpddclose-uphardware

Evaluation Prompt
